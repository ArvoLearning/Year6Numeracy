BUSINESS BASICS

Revenue, Cost, Profit & Loss — the language of business

⏱ 10 mins

Every business — even a small one like a lemonade stand — uses these four key ideas. Tap each card to learn what it means.

💸

COST

How much it costs YOU to make or buy something.

💰

REVENUE

How much money you EARN from selling something. (Also called income.)

📈

PROFIT

When Revenue is MORE than Cost. You made money!

📉

LOSS

When Cost is MORE than Revenue. You lost money.

PROFIT / LOSS = REVENUE − COST

💡 Quick check: If Revenue = $50 and Cost = $30, is that a profit or loss? Click to check → PROFIT of $20! ($50 − $30 = $20)

REAL-WORLD SCENARIOS

Set up and solve multi-step profit & loss problems

⏱ 15 mins
PROBLEM 1 · 👕 T-Shirt Shop
A shop buys 10 t-shirts for $12 each. They sell each t-shirt for $25. How much profit do they make if they sell all 10 t-shirts?
Cost
unit cost × number
Revenue
unit price × number
Profit / Loss
revenue − cost
Cost = 10 × $12 = $120.00
Revenue = 10 × $25 = $250.00
Profit = $250.00 − $120.00 =
PROFIT of $130.00 📈
PROBLEM 2 · 🍪 Cookie Stand (Tricky!)
You bake 20 cookies. Ingredients cost $0.50 per cookie. You sell 15 cookies for $1.50 each. Did you make a profit or a loss, and by how much?
⚠️ Watch out — you made 20 cookies but only SOLD 15! Cost is based on what you MADE; revenue is based on what you SOLD.
Cost (all 20 made)
$0.50 × 20
Revenue (15 sold)
$1.50 × 15
Profit / Loss
revenue − cost
Cost = 20 × $0.50 = $10.00 (cost of ALL cookies made)
Revenue = 15 × $1.50 = $22.50 (only the ones SOLD)
Profit = $22.50 − $10.00 =
PROFIT of $12.50 📈 (even with 5 unsold cookies!)

What's the point of all this maths?

⏱ 5 mins

What is the main goal for most businesses when it comes to profit and loss?

✅ Correct! The main goal is to make a PROFIT. Businesses need Revenue to be greater than Cost so they can pay their workers, buy more materials, and grow. A business that keeps making a loss can't survive for long!
